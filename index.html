How it works

1

College Issues

The university digitally signs academic credentials (transcripts, identity, achievements) with their private key. The public key is embedded in the credential for self-contained verification.

2

Student Holds

Credentials live in the student's wallet — not in a central database. Students control what they share using toggle switches, and can prove claims (like GPA > 8.0) without revealing exact values.

3

Third Party Verifies

Employers, scholarship boards, or any verifier enters a code or scans a QR. The system checks the issuer's digital signature and validates any ZKP range proofs — instant, mathematical trust.

Built for trust

🔐

Cryptographic Signatures

ECDSA P-256 digital signatures via Web Crypto API. Every credential is mathematically bound to the issuing institution — tamper-proof by design.

🎛️

Selective Disclosure

Students choose exactly which fields to share. Show your major without revealing your GPA, or prove enrollment without exposing personal details.

🧮

Zero-Knowledge Proofs

Prove your GPA is above a threshold without revealing the exact number. SHA-256 commitment binds the range claim to the signed credential.

📱

QR Code Sharing

Generate a QR code for any verifiable presentation. Verifiers scan and get instant results — perfect for in-person verification.

⏱️

Expiry & Revocation

Credentials expire naturally, and issuers can revoke them. The verifier checks both before accepting any proof.

🏛️

Multi-Issuer Trust

Support credentials from multiple institutions. Each issuer registers their own key pair — verifiers trust the cryptography, not a single database.
